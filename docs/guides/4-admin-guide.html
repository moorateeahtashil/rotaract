1. Getting into the Admin Portal

Log in at Sign In. If your account has an Admin or Super Admin role you'll land on the Admin Portal (/admin). Everything is reached from the left sidebar, grouped into Overview, Content, People, Program, Media & Resources, Insights, and System.

Most sections work the same way: a list of items with Add, an Edit (pencil), and Delete (trash) action, and a pop-up form for the details.

2. Roles & who can do what

RoleCan do
Super AdminEverything, including managing other admins, deleting users, and creating Super Admins.
AdminThe full Admin Portal. Can assign roles up to (not above) their own level.
Board MemberMember portal + elevated rights on certain content; no admin sidebar.
MemberFull member portal; listed in the directory.
Prospective MemberMember portal (incl. attendance) while earning membership; sees progress; not in directory.
An admin can never grant a role more powerful than their own. Only a Super Admin can create another Super Admin.

3. People

Members

The central roster of everyone on the platform.

  • Add — invite a new person by email (choose their role); they receive an invitation link.
  • Approve — promote a pending applicant/prospect to full Member.
  • Edit roles — change a person's system role (Admin) and org role (Member / Board / Prospective).
  • Delete — remove a user.

Applications

Submissions from the public Join form. Review the details, then Approve (which invites and onboards them as a Member and emails them) or Reject with optional notes.

Board Members & Committees

Manage the board roster with positions, and committees with a chair chosen from board members.

4. Attendance & Membership Requirements

People → Attendance is where you record participation and track who's ready to become a member.

Mark Attendance

1
Open the Mark Attendance tab and choose an event.
2
Tick everyone who attended (members and prospects). Changes save instantly.

This is in addition to members self-checking-in by QR — use it to correct or add attendance (e.g. for someone who couldn't scan, or after an event has ended).

By Person

See each person's attendance. Prospective members show their requirement progress; when complete they get a green Ready badge and a one-click Promote button.

Requirements

Click Requirements to set how many of each event type a prospect must attend (e.g. 4 Club Meetings, 2 Fellowships, 2 Projects) plus optional project-lead roles. Set 0 to ignore a type. These thresholds are fully editable.

5. Program

Events

Create events with an image, date/time, location, capacity, status, and an event type. Each event has a QR button that generates its attendance code (see workflow §9).

Event Types

The categories used for events and attendance requirements. Six core types are locked and cannot be deleted: Project, Fellowship, Professional Development, Club Meeting, Club Assembly, Board Meeting. You can add your own additional types.

Projects

Service projects. Creating a project also creates a linked Project-type event automatically, so the project is attendable and counts toward membership requirements.

News & Announcements

News — public blog posts with a featured image and publish toggle. Announcements — messages shown in the member portal, with a priority, optional expiry, and publish toggle.

6. Media & Resources

Gallery

Create albums and upload multiple images to each.

Resources

Upload documents and set an access level: public, member-only, or board-only.

FAQs

Add Q&A entries with a category, sort order, and visibility toggle — visible FAQs appear on the public FAQ page, grouped by category.

Sponsors

Manage the sponsoring Rotary club(s) shown on the public Sponsors page — name, district, logo, description, president, website, socials.

7. Content & Site Settings

Site Settings

Club name, tagline, hero banner image, logo, next-meeting info, social links, footer, and contact details.

Rotary Highlights

The cards in the "What is Rotary?" section on the homepage.

Legal Pages

Edit the Terms and Privacy pages.

8. Insights & System

Analytics

A live snapshot of people, program, and content counts, plus recent sign-ups.

Users & Roles

Search any user and change their primary role. Assigning a member/board role also creates their member record; choosing "No role" removes portal access.

Audit Log

A chronological record of administrative activity — who did what, and when.

9. Common Workflows

Onboard a new member

1
Members → Add (or approve from Applications).
2
Enter name, email, role → they get an invite email.

Run attendance for an event

1
Events → click the event's QR button to generate its code.
2
Display the QR (or the text code) at the venue — members scan it to check in.
3
Afterwards, fix any gaps in Attendance → Mark Attendance.
Codes expire after 24 hours, and only one is active per event — generate a fresh one each time.

Promote a prospect to member

1
Set the thresholds once in Attendance → Requirements.
2
Record attendance as prospects participate.
3
When a prospect shows Ready (green), click Promote.

10. On your phone

The Admin Portal is mobile-friendly. Tap the menu (☰) icon in the top bar to open the admin navigation drawer; tap any section to go there. All forms, lists, and the attendance tools adapt to small screens.
